# Digging out: revenue & self-financing levers (cited)

Companion to `council-finance-critique.md`. Evidence for how a council could raise new
revenue / become more self-financing **without** a central bailout — separating sound,
productive, asset-building strategies from the speculative bets that bankrupted other
councils. Confidence labels: **[Verified]** primary/authoritative source fetched;
**[Reported]** credible secondary; **[Illustrative]** scenario, not a forecast.

## The decisive line: productive assets vs. speculative bets
- **[Verified] Speculative commercial-property-for-yield is the failure mode.** Woking
  issued a Section 114 (June 2023) with ~£1.2bn debt and >£600m of impaired development
  assets; Thurrock ran ~£1.5bn-scale losses on borrowed energy/property bets; Croydon and
  Slough also failed. In response the **PWLB barred borrowing to buy assets "primarily for
  yield" (Nov 2020)** and CIPFA ruled it imprudent (Dec 2021).
  [Public Finance — Woking](https://www.publicfinance.co.uk/news/2023/06/woking-issues-section-114-notice),
  [Arlingclose](https://www.arlingclose.com/insights/how-regulators-failed-to-stop-local-government-failures).
  *The lesson: productive, service-linked, income-generating assets remain permitted;
  leveraged financial speculation does not.*

## Sound / mixed levers

1. **[Verified] Council housing for rent + temporary-accommodation avoidance — SOUND.**
   The 2018 removal of the HRA borrowing cap reopened direct council housebuilding against
   rental income. The demand-side saving is large: England spent **£2.84bn on temporary
   accommodation in 2024/25 (+25% in a year, +118% over five years)**, with councils' own
   unsubsidised share rising from 7.1% (2009/10) to **50.6% (2024/25)** as Housing Benefit
   subsidy is frozen at 90% of Jan-2011 rates. Owning rental homes substitutes an asset for
   a high-cost liability.
   [LGA — HRA cap removal](https://www.local.gov.uk/publications/housing-revenue-account-cap-removal),
   [Shelter — TA bill](https://england.shelter.org.uk/media/press_release/bill_for_homeless_accommodation_soars_by_25_hitting_28_bn_).

2. **[Verified] Municipal renewable *generation* — MIXED (works, but don't over-borrow).**
   Warrington's council-owned solar farms paid a **£2.4m dividend (2023)** with a projected
   ~£150m 30-year surplus — but Warrington's heavy borrowing also drew S114-adjacent
   scrutiny. Retail energy *supply* is a clear failure: Robin Hood Energy lost £34m+,
   Bristol Energy ~£35–46m, ~£74m+ across council energy firms.
   [Generation](https://www.warrington-worldwide.co.uk/2023/09/19/cabinet-approves-2-4m-profit-dividend-from-councils-solar-farm-investments/),
   [Supply failures](https://www.taxpayersalliance.com/council_energy_firms_lose_114_million).

3. **[Verified] Community Municipal Investment / local climate bonds — SOUND but modest.**
   Eight councils (West Berkshire, Warrington, Islington, Camden, Cotswold, Telford &
   Wrekin, Westminster, Lewisham) raised **~£6.4m** from residents since 2020 at ~1.2–4.85%
   — cheaper than PWLB. Supplementary finance, not a budget fix.
   [Zero Carbon Accelerator — CMI](https://zerocarbonaccelerator.london/green-funding-and-finance/community-municipal-investment-cmi-local-climate-bonds-lcbs/).

4. **[Verified] Land value capture (CIL/S106) — MIXED, under-exploited.** Authorities held
   **>£8bn unspent developer contributions**; of ~£12.4bn land-value uplift on new build
   (2014/15) only ~£2.8bn was captured — **~75% (£9.3bn) went uncaptured**; only ~48% of
   eligible authorities charge CIL.
   [HCLG Committee — Land Value Capture](https://publications.parliament.uk/pa/cm201719/cmselect/cmcomloc/766/76606.htm).

5. **[Verified] Insourcing to recapture contractor margin — SOUND (soft figures).** APSE
   surveys: ~73% of authorities insourced ≥1 service; two-thirds reported savings, 78%
   greater flexibility. Hard generalisable "% margin recaptured" figures are thin.
   [APSE / London Assembly](https://www.london.gov.uk/about-us/londonassembly/meetings/documents/s78359/04b%20APSE%20-%20the%20case%20for%20insourcing%20-%20May%202019.pdf).

6. **[Verified] Community Wealth Building / Preston — SOUND (best-evidenced).** Locally
   retained anchor spend rose **5% → 18.2% (~£74m)** in Preston, 39% → 79.2% (~£200m)
   across Lancashire. Note: this is procurement *redirection*, not net new council revenue.
   [CLES — The Preston Model](https://cles.org.uk/the-preston-model/).

## Devon / Exeter specifics
- **[Verified] Exeter City Living (the council's for-sale development company) is being
  wound up (Oct 2023)** after >£4.5m cumulative losses, owing the council ~£10.1m (only
  £754k repaid), having delivered **just 22 homes** against a 675 target; an independent
  review called it "an expensive, high-risk delivery mechanism Exeter cannot afford to
  continue." **This is the key local lesson: a for-sale development vehicle carries market
  risk and lost money — distinct from an HRA *landlord* model holding homes for rent.**
  [Exeter Observer](https://exeterobserver.org/2023/10/06/exeter-city-living-4.5-million-losses-10-million-owed-exeter-city-council-resulting-sale-value-clifton-hill-development-site-not-cover-company-debts-liveable-exeter-vision-doubt-councillors-evade-responsibility-failure/).
- **[Reported]** Exeter's more defensible self-financing routes: HRA-funded direct council
  homebuilding (~500 homes/10yr target, 4,800-home retrofit) and the £3.5m Water Lane
  smart-grid/storage project. Devon CC's renewable footprint is small (community solar at
  Great Moor House); no large Devon commercial-property-for-yield strategy found.

## Honest framing for a skeptical reader
None of these is a substitute for core funding. CMI bonds (~£6.4m nationally) and Preston's
£74m are real but modest. The credible claim is **diversified, lower-risk asset and income
building plus stopping leakage** — *not* "self-financing without central government," and
emphatically *not* leveraged speculation. Lean on HRA rental assets and TA-cost avoidance,
not for-sale development companies.
